What is the A-to-z Guarantee?
All products sold and fulfilled by third-party (marketplace) sellers are protected
by Amazon’s A-to-Z Guarantee. Read on to understand more on the claim process, eligibility
and restrictions.
To access in your preferred language, locate the country flag on the top banner. Use
the drop down to select the language.
For information on how to request an A-to-z Guarantee refund, visit:
Request an A-to-z Guarantee Refund
What is Amazon’s A-to-z Guarantee?
The Amazon A-to-Z Guarantee protects customers when they purchase items sold and shipped
by a third-party seller who provides their own customer service directly to you. Customers
can file an A-to-Z claim provided they are eligible and meet the requirements listed
below. Learn more on how to file an A-to-Z claim at
Request an A-to-z Guarantee Refund
.
A-to-z Guarantee eligibility
A customer is eligible to file an A-to-z Guarantee claim if:
You have purchased physical goods which was sold and shipped by a third-party seller
on the Amazon.in marketplace.
You have contacted the seller through your Amazon account and waited 48 hours for
a reply.
At least one of the conditions listed in the below table applies.
A-to-z Guarantee eligibility for Delivery Issues
A-to-z Guarantee eligibility for Item Condition issues
Customer hasn’t received their package and 3 days have passed since the estimated
delivery date or the tracking shows a delivery confirmation, whichever is sooner.
The item received is damaged, defective, or doesn't match the description given. Customer
has returned it in line with the Amazon.in Returns Policy, but hasn't received the
relevant refund, or the refund amount was incorrect.
In these cases, customer must do the following:
Contact the seller within the applicable return window mentioned in the Amazon.in
Returns Policy. To know more about returning orders placed with third-party sellers,
refer to
Return Orders placed with a Third-party Seller
Self-ship the return within 14 days after the return with the seller has been arranged.
Criteria for filing A-to-z Guarantee claims
You will need to have contacted the Seller through your Amazon account and waited
48 hours for a reply.
To file a claim for an item not received
-Wait for 3 business days past the maximum estimated delivery date (EDD) is over.
To file a claim for an item they received that was damaged, defective or materially
different
- Wait until the minimum estimated delivery date (EDD).
Restrictions for the A-to-z Guarantee
The following items and situations aren't covered under the Amazon A-to-Z Guarantee:
Digital Merchandise
Credit card payments where issuing bank has initiated a chargeback.
Note: A-to-z Guarantee claims can take up to 1 week to process.
Frequently Asked Questions
Is there a particular timeline until when a claim can be filed?
You can file a claim up to 90 days after estimated delivery date (EDD) using the
self-service option.
How to check the status of a claim?
Please follow the steps mentioned below
Go to
Your Orders
page.
Locate your order in the list. Select
View Order Details.
Click on the
Problem with order
button.
Check the status of the claim under section Refund Request Status.
For more information, visit
View the status of your A-to-z refund
help page.
What if the customer received a damaged/ defective / wrong Amazon Business order?
Please inform the seller of the issue within 7 days of receiving the item to initiate
the return process.
Customer has already contacted the seller. What should they do to request a refund?
Wait for a response from the seller. If there is no response after 48 hours, the customer
is eligible to request a refund.
How many refund requests is a customer eligible for?
You are limited to a lifetime maximum of 75 refund requests. Withdrawn requests don't
count towards this maximum.
